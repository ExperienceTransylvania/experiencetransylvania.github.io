COVID-19: What you should know before travelling to Romania

Starting May 17th 2021 Romania's borders are open for travel for all citizens of the world. If you are fully vaccinated (one or both doses as applicable) and more than 10 days have passed since, you are not required to quarantine or take a test no matter where you come from. You need to have the relevant papers (stating your vaccination or negative PCR test) printed or in electronic format (in English). Upon entry (via land or flight) local authorities will check these so queues and delays are possible. You can find up-to-date information on EU’s official website for reopening borders and travel between member states.

DRACULA’S TRANSYLVANIA
- 12 DAYS -
GRAND TOUR

With Transylvanian local guide Nora Vintila & great-grandnephew of Bram Stoker, international best selling author Dacre Stoker


Nora meets you at the airport. After a relax & spa afternoon, Dacre introduces you to how his uncle wrote the novel Dracula.

Day 1

Visit Bucharest’s old town, uncovering secrets from the communist dictatorship. Head towards the Carpathians in the afternoon.

Day 2

Hike up to the Poienari Fortress, drive on the impressive road of the Transfagarasan and arrive to Sibiu later this evening.

Day 3

After shopping and strolling around in charming Sibiu, we depart for Sighisoara. But not before we stop by in one of the colorful Saxon villages.

Day 4


Some free time in Sighisoara. Later we head towards the region of the Borgo Pass and stop for a wine tasting in the idyllic setting of the Transylvanian highlands.

Day 5

Start your day with a morning dip in the fresh mountain lake! Later on, we taste a variety of local cheese at a local shepherd's farm.

Day 6

Hiking day! We head towards the extinct volcano site of the mountain top where Bram Stoker placed Dracula’s fictional castle.

Day 7

A local blacksmith visit, a Transylvanian beer tasting and a renaissance dinner at a Szekely manor, to the sound of live music.

Day 8


Meet Katharina Siegel, Vlad Dracula’s mistress, in the quaint town of Brasov or Kronstadt as it's called by the German locals.

Day 9

Picnic in the Carpathians, rosé wine and pine bark cheese. Candlelit dinner with a Victorian touch, on Bran Castle's premises.

Day 10

A view of royal Peles Castle and a visit to Snagov Monastery, alleged burial site of Vlad Dracula. Romanian dances for dinner.

Day 11

Your local guide Nora Vintila and your Dracula-host Dacre Stoker say "LA REVEDERE"! and see you off to the airport.

Day 12
