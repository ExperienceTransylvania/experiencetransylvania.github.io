COVID-19: What you should know before travelling to Romania

Starting May 17th 2021 Romania's borders are open for travel for all citizens of the world. If you are fully vaccinated (one or both doses as applicable) and more than 10 days have passed since, you are not required to quarantine or take a test no matter where you come from. You need to have the relevant papers (stating your vaccination or negative PCR test) printed or in electronic format (in English). Upon entry (via land or flight) local authorities will check these so queues and delays are possible. You can find up-to-date information on EU’s official website for reopening borders and travel between member states.

DRACULA’S TRANSYLVANIA
- 12 DAYS -
GRAND TOUR

With Transylvanian local guide/tour manager Nora Vintila and great grand nephew of Bram Stoker, international best selling author Dacre Stoker
Price: from 2100 €/pers (excl flight)
Duration: 12 days
Group: 10-20 pers

Meet local guide and welcome to Romania. Explore the relax & spa area of the hotel.

Day 1

Visit Bucharest’s old town. In the afternoon, we leave Bucharest and head towards the Carpathian Mountains

Day 2

Hike up to the Poienari Fortress, drive on the impressive road of the Transfagarasan and arrive to Sibiu later this evening.

Day 3

After shopping and strolling around in charming Sibiu, we depart for Sighisoara. But not before we stop by in one of the colorful Saxon villages.

Day 4


Some free time in Sighisoara. Later we head towards the region of the Borgo Pass and stop for a wine tasting in the idyllic setting of the Transylvanian highlands.

Day 5

A morning dip in the mountain lake, lunch is served at a shepherds farm, where we witness the cheese making process and taste a variety of local cheese. Crossing the Carpathian chain.

Day 6

This is a hiking day. We leave early in the morning and the destination is the extinct volcano site of the mountain top where Bram Stoker placed Dracula’s fictional castle. Evening? A relaxing movie night introduced by Dacre.

Day 7

Leaving Calimani National Park behind, we head now southwards where we visit local blacksmith, check in at a nobility house, feast renaissance dinner served in the manor salon, in the sound of classical live music.

Day 8


Arriving to Brasov. Nora takes us on a guided walk through the old town, including Black Church and the house of Katharina, known as Vlad Dracula’s mistress. Move on towards Bran where Dacre will explain what he discovered.

Day 9

An excursion to the higher meadows of the Carpathian and we will taste the traditional Transylvanian cheese matured in pine bark. Ok, back to our hotel in Bran to get ready for the Bran Castle. Bring your gothic / victorian / vampire accessories!

Day 10

Head towards Bucharest. On the way, we stop and admire the exterior of the beautiful Peles Castle and visit Snagov Monastery, one of the alleged burial sites of Vlad Dracula. Later in Bucharest, it is time to relax with a traditional Romanian folklore dance show.

Day 11

After a last breakfast on Romanian grounds, it’s time to depart towards the airport. Your local guide Nora Vintila and your Dracula-host Dacre Stoker say "LA REVEDERE"!

Team

Our local guides are just a chat away from creating your most genuine travel experience, in Europe's most mysterious, yet most welcoming place. We are a family business based in Transylvania. We've been designing unforgettable travel experiences for guests from all over the world since 2004.

Gloria Andersson

Our Transylvanian born and raised travel professional, CEO of the travel agency Experience Transylvania since 2012. Living in Sweden for half the year and in Transylvania for the other half, you could say her life is similar to the life of the iconic storks living in the picturesque nests that decorate almost every Transylvanian village. Since 2004 she's been a tour leader in Central and Eastern Europe, with a focus on her home country, Romania. Gloria designs our tours in a service-minded way, aiming for the most genuine experiences.

Dacre Stoker

Our go-to person when it comes to anything realted to Dracula. Dacre is the great-grand-nephew of the author of Dracula, Bram Stoker. As the literary genius seems to run in the family, Dacre is a best-selling author of both fictional and non-fictional publications. His research of Bram's notes started 15+ years ago and has revealed many interesting details about the iconic gothic novel. Dacre leads our tours in the footsteps of the fictional figure Count Dracula, based on a thorough study of his uncle's work as a writer.

Nora Vintila

Our language talent and certified guide in Romania. Tour and event manager, artist and history aficionado. For Nora, even a walk in the park is almost impossible without her sharing some lesser known facts about the surroundings. An experienced cruise director on European waterways for over 10 years, Nora is knowledgeable and enthusiastic about culture, history gossip, and insider-information. A hiking day with Nora can end up around the bonfire, over a warm drink, with a guitar and a repertoire of joyful songs.
