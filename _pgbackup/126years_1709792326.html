Literary tours: Dracula in England & Scotland


with Dacre Stoker, great-grandnephew of Bram Stoker


UK & Ireland

This is a sample itinerary focused on England and Scotland. For the Ireland trip, updates are being made currently. Please click on the respective buttons below, to get the most updated version of the itineraries in the UK and in Ireland
We develop and update the itineraries periodically, to keep up with local conditions, but also to bring in the newest literary and historical research results. We optimize the program with the guests’ comfort and enjoyment in mind. That’s why you might find features in the program below, that are not reflected in the concrete itinerary. Please view the itinerary below as mere inspiration, and check out the updated itinerary that is ready- to-book by clicking here.
Dacre Stoker, the great-grandnephew of novelist Bram Stoker, is organizing literary expeditions to places that played an important role in his ancestor’s life and career as a writer.
For the past 15+ years, Dacre has made a thorough analysis of his uncle's work and discovered exciting details in his research. During these trips we will visit places that played a big role in Bram Stoker's life, in his research and writing of the novel. Some of the tours take us to the coastal town of Whitby, with its iconic abbey ruins. Other itineraries are set to Cruden Bay in Scotland, the picturesque fishing village where Bram wrote most of the novel. And some of our literary travels take us to Dublin, Ireland, to explore Bram Stoker’s early life and career, as well as richness of early Irish folklore and how it influenced gothic literature. There will be several interesting stops on our journey, to provide a perspective of the history and culture of the areas we visit.


Good to know before the tour!

Some of the hikes in Whitby and Cruden Bay, as well as the outdoor experiences in Ireland can be strenuous and will require waterproof, above the ankle, footwear. May’s weather in these regions are unpredictable, so waterproof rain gear and layers are recommended.
