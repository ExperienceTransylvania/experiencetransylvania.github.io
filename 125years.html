125 Years of Dracula:
England & Scotland


Over the course of this trip, we will visit the locations that played a large role in Bram’s life, his research and writing of "Dracula". Absorb the atmosphere, learn the history, and experience the dramatic beauty of multiple locations, together with knowledgeable guides. We will visit the coastal town of Whitby in Yorkshire, England that so inspired Bram Stoker that he set chapters 6-8 of "Dracula" in this location. We will also explore Cruden Bay, Scotland, the quaint fishing village that Bram first visited in 1893, which became the location of his 13 summer holidays between 1893 and 1910, and is where he wrote most of Dracula. There will be other exciting stops on our journey to provide a perspective of the history and culture in the area.

Good to know before the tour!

Some of the hikes in Whitby and Cruden Bay are strenuous and will require waterproof, above the ankle, footwear. May’s weather in Yorkshire and coastal Scotland can be unpredictable, so waterproof rain gear and layers are recommended.

With Transylvanian local guide/tour manager Nora Vintila and great grand nephew of Bram Stoker, international best selling author Dacre Stoker.

Day 1


Saturday May 21st / Manchester - York - Whitby
York Dungeon & Bram’s Whitby Holiday
Get together and meet Dacre Stoker, Nora Vintila and your fellow travelers at the Central Station in Manchester. Leaving Manchester for Whitby, we enjoy the genuine English countryside during an exciting coach ride. We will stop for a short energizing coffee break along the way towards our adventurous journey. We continue with an exciting visit to the York Dungeons and the historic York Minster, which will prepare us for the upcoming thrill of arriving in Whitby. Lunch is served at a popular local restaurant after which we continue our way to Whitby. We reach Whitby in the afternoon and check in to our hotel. After a short time to relax we enjoy a welcome drink & snack. Dacre leads a leisurely evening walk thru the town to get acquainted with the gothic atmosphere that attracted his famous relative, Bram Stoker, during his holiday in 1890. We walk up the 199 steps to the St Mary’s churchyard to the location where Lucy Westenra had her encounter with Count Dracula. We are back at the hotel by dusk. Dinner is served at the hotel, afterwards join Dacre and Nora for a fireside chat about Transylvanian folklore and Whitby related readings from Dracula.

Day 2


Sunday May 22nd / Whitby
Dracula sites in Whitby
We start the day with a walking tour to the iconic sights mentioned in Dracula: Royal Crescent, Whitby Abbey, St. Mary’s Church, 199 steps, Whitby Train station, Bram Stoker bench, and the Whitby Museum. Together with Dacre we are guided by a very knowledgeable local guide. Lunch will be in town at the Duke of York Pub, right at the base of the 199 steps, under Whitby Abbey. There will be free time for shopping after lunch. Back at the hotel, we immerse ourselves into the Stoker writing spirit, during Dacre’s audio visual presentation “Bram Stoker’s Whitby, Influences and Inspiration”. Witness Bram’s research notes coming to life and learn how real places and events in Whitby were featured in the novel Dracula. Explore the local cuisine and enjoy dinner on your own or in the company of fellow travelers.

Day 3


Monday May 23rd / Whitby
Free time in Whitby


Take your time to enjoy Whitby at own pace. We recommend other locations to visit in Whitby: Whitby Brewery, Dracula Experience, Coastguard Museum, shopping for souvenirs, etc. We have lunch together and visit the Whitby Jet Museum. Jet is a black mineral, it was formed from the wood of a type of ‘monkey puzzle’ tree over 135 million years ago. It is very light and can be very highly polished. It is highly prized for making jewelry and ornaments and is found throughout the North York Moors. Whitby is famous for the manufacture of jet jewelry, particularly so in the last part of the 19th century when Queen Victoria popularized this type of jewelry. Although it has no direct connection to Dracula, this museum is well worth seeing, as it really is a hidden gem of the town. Explore the local cuisine and enjoy dinner on your own or in the company of fellow travelers.

Day 4


Tuesday May 24th / Whitby - Edinburgh
Edinburgh whiskey and history

On the way to Edinburgh, we make a coffee stop by Alnwick Castle, iconic for its Harry Potter setting. We continue our way northwards and reach Edinburgh in the early afternoon. Lunch is served at the award-winning restaurant of the Scotch Whiskey Experience, followed by an exciting virtual tour of a distillery including tasting a dram of whiskey. For those who wish, there is enough time to also the imposing Edinburgh Castle to get an overview of Scotland history and culture (entrance fee not included, your tour manager can help you with purchasing the tickets). In order to thereafter experience a real distillery, we can recommend a bunch that are within reasonable distance from our hotel. After checking in at a central hotel, enjoy some free time in Edinburgh, for shopping and dinner on your own.

Day 5


Wednesday May 25th / Edinburgh - Cruden Bay
Scotland's East Coast

A unique experience awaits, stepping down into the hidden history of Edinburgh, at the Real Mary King’s Close. On this one-hour tour we will experience over 400 years of history and discover the stories from myths and legends to the deadly plague epidemic, and a famous royal visitor as we delve into Edinburgh’s dark past. We then travel from Edinburgh to Cruden Bay, with exciting stops on the way, such as Dundee and Dunottar Castel, where we also have lunch (depending on the weather, Dunottar Castle can be closed on stormy days, if this is the case, we will visit another, more inland, castle). We arrive at the Kilmarnock Arms Hotel in Cruden Bay in the afternoon, where Bram Stoker himself was a guest in 1893. We will be joined by local author Mike Shepherd, who has written a book about Bram Stoker’s summer visits to this area, and another on the history of Slains Castle, for an informal orientation walk around town. Dinner is served at the hotel, followed by an audio-visual presentation with Dacre and Mike about “Bram Stoker’s Cruden Bay Experiences”.

Day 6

Tuesday May 26th / Cruden Bay
125 years of Dracula


Today we celebrate 125 years since the novel Dracula was published. This calls for a champagne breakfast! We then start off at the Kilmarnock Arms Hotel for an exciting beach walk with Mike Shepherd which recreates Bram Stoker’s daily “inspirational writing” walks. Enjoy first-hand the locations Bram frequented while writing Dracula and mentioned in two of his novels based in Cruden Bay. The Mystery of the Sea 1902 and The Watters Mou 1895. Back to the hotel for lunch, after which we continue our walk to the ruins of Slains Castle. We have a fantastic view of the facade, while Dacre and Mike reveal images of the octagonal room featured in chapter 2 of Dracula (access within the ruins is prohibited due to site safety risks). Enjoy some free time for exploring Cruden Bay at your pace, or to re-read pages of the novel to get into the atmosphere of the celebration. A festive dinner is served at the Kilmarnock Arms Hotel and the entire evening is dedicated to the celebration of 125 years since the novel Dracula was published.

Day 7

Wednesday May 27th / Fyvie, Glasgow
Fyvie Castle and coastal nature


We check out from Cruden Bay and drive southwards. We stop for admiring a natural wonder on the coast at Bullers of Buchan, and then continue with a visit at picturesque Fyvie Castle, where ghosts, legends and folklore are all woven into the tapestry of the 800-year-old history of this place. The coach ride continues to Glasgow, with lunch on the way. This is where our Dracula jubilee host Dacre Stoker and our Transylvania expert Nora Vintila say goodbye. We check in at a central hotel, from where you may explore the local cuisine and culture of Glasgow this evening.

Day 8

Wednesday May 28th / Glasgow
Last day


Check out from the hotel after breakfast or continue your stay in Scotland with more cultural activities on your own. La revedere, see you in Transylvania next time!
